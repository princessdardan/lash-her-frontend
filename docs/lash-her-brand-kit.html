01 / Brand Position

Quiet luxury, high precision, personal transformation.

Lash Her should feel intimate, refined, and expert-led. The current website now implements a white-first editorial system with exact dark image overlays, a fixed glass navigation state, Bebas Neue display typography, bold Inter support copy, structured service cards, and calmer conversion moments. This brand kit is synchronized to that live direction across web, booking flows, training material, social graphics, and print collateral.

Brand essence

Luxury lash artistry with a personal, mentorship-driven tone. The brand should feel calm, confident, feminine, and exacting.

Primary audience

Clients seeking premium lash work in Toronto and students seeking serious, hands-on lash education.

Design principle

Use restraint first. Let compressed editorial typography, whitespace, photography, thin rules, and a few high-contrast accents create the sense of value.

03 / Typography

Condensed, editorial, readable.

Use only open-license or Google-hosted fonts. The implemented site now uses Bebas Neue for display, heading, smallcaps, and serif utility roles, with Inter for readable body and interface text. The prior serif-led direction is retired from the live frontend.

Bespoke Lash Artistry
Elevating beauty through detail.

Font roles

  • Bebas Neue: hero headlines, section titles, card titles, labels, nav accents, logo text, gallery headings, and status pages.
  • Inter: paragraph copy, buttons, forms, captions, pricing, booking instructions, training details, validation messages, and email-friendly UI text.
  • Weight contrast: use regular-weight Bebas Neue for display scale, then lean on Inter 700 for hero support copy, navigation CTAs, form labels, helper text, and portable text emphasis.
  • Legacy note: avoid reintroducing the previous script or serif-led font stack unless a future redesign explicitly chooses it.

04 / Logo & Identity Use

Let the name behave like a signature.

Primary wordmark

Use “Lash Her” as a condensed editorial wordmark in Bebas Neue with a slight italic feel when needed. Keep “by Nataliea” smaller, letter-spaced, uppercase, and secondary. The brand should feel personal without becoming casual.

Monogram

Use LH as a simple ligature or stacked monogram for favicon, social profile, wax seal graphic, certificate mark, and booking confirmation assets.

Clear space

Minimum clear space should equal the height of the “L” in the wordmark. Never place the logo over busy lash photography without a dark overlay.

Preferred lockup: Lash Her in Bebas Neue with generous tracking and “by Nataliea” in smaller Bebas Neue uppercase with 0.32em tracking. Use Midnight Espresso on white or Dusty Silk, Dusty Silk on Midnight Espresso, or Antique Champagne on Black Cherry.

06 / Photography & Art Direction

Close detail, soft contrast, editorial restraint.

Client imagery

Use close crops of eyes, lash texture, clean skin, soft shadows, and minimal backgrounds. Prioritize real client results over generic beauty stock.

Training imagery

Show hands, tools, student practice, clean workstations, certificates, and instructor guidance. Keep images warm and precise.

Treatment

Use warm highlights, low saturation, soft grain, espresso overlays, and dark multiply overlays on hero images. Avoid heavy pink filters, harsh ring-light glare, and cluttered treatment-room backgrounds.

08 / Layout Rules

Use whitespace as a luxury signal.

Spacing

  • Use 24px as the base spacing unit for cards and section interiors.
  • Use section shells with 44px mobile, 64px tablet, and 96px desktop vertical rhythm.
  • Keep content constrained to 1180px with 24–32px side gutters.

Shape and depth

  • Use the Tailwind radius base of 18px for default UI surfaces.
  • Use 18px radii for fields, dropdown menus, and navigation viewports.
  • Use 24px radii for editorial cards and gallery images.
  • Use 28px radii for soft and dark panels; prefer thin borders over heavy shadows.

Navigation

  • Header is fixed at the top with a centered logo, desktop nav pinned left, and CMS-driven CTAs pinned right.
  • The first CMS-driven CTA is the only primary filled nav button; do not hardcode “Book Now” as a rule.
  • At rest, the header is transparent over hero imagery. After 50px scroll, it becomes bg-lh-primary/30 with backdrop blur, brand line, and soft shadow.
  • Mobile uses a left hamburger that opens a right-side sheet with expandable menu sections.

Forms

  • Use white fields inside Dusty Silk soft panels.
  • Use Royal Mulberry focus rings and Black Cherry error states.
  • Use bold Inter labels and concise helper text; success states stay quiet and neutral.

09 / Motion & Interaction

Motion should reveal, not perform.

Block reveal

Most CMS layout blocks use a scroll reveal wrapper with reduced-motion support. Hero sections skip this entrance treatment so the first impression loads immediately.

Header transition

The fixed header uses a 300ms transition between transparent and Mulberry glass states. Avoid hiding the header on scroll; the current pattern keeps navigation available.

Utility motion

Use .mask-gradient for horizontal fade masks and .animate-fade-in-overlay for slow overlay entrances. Keep motion subtle and accessibility-aware.

11 / Do & Don’t

Execution standards

Do

  • Use oversized editorial headlines.
  • Keep body copy readable and practical.
  • Use Antique Champagne as a premium accent.
  • Use authentic lash photography.
  • Make booking CTAs obvious but calm.

Don’t

  • Use neon pink, rose-gold gradients, or glitter effects.
  • Overcrowd pages with badges and decorative icons.
  • Use script fonts for long text.
  • Use low-contrast champagne text on white.
  • Make every section dark; contrast loses meaning.

13 / Recommended Website Direction

How the new system fits the current site

Homepage

Use a full-bleed photographic hero with from-lh-shadow/80 via-lh-accent/70 to-lh-primary/60, oversized Bebas Neue heading, bold Inter support copy, and Antique Champagne luxury CTA.

Training section

Use editorial-card course tiles with Royal Mulberry hierarchy, Antique Champagne “Most Popular” treatment, icon badges, accent rules, and one clear application CTA per card.

Gallery

Use the implemented coverflow direction: large vertical crops, 24px image radii, soft shadow, drag affordance, subtle perspective, and quiet dot pagination.

Contact and booking

Use section-shell layouts, Dusty Silk soft panels, white 18px form fields, Royal Mulberry focus states, Black Cherry errors, and a dark contact card for location, hours, email, and booking links.
